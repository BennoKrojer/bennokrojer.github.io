Reflections on the role of benchmarking in AI Progress

Creating a meaningful and long-lasting benchmark or dataset requires so many factors to work together which are usually not as acknowledged as they should be:

  1. Task formulation: Is it a meaningful task while also being easy for diverse models to quickly set up?
  2. Difficulty: Is it hard enough to be intriguing while also not getting people stuck with low numbers?
  3. Crowdsourcing: How to train workers especially for harder tasks?
  4. Publishing and getting people interested: leaderboard, challenges, good paper and analysis of phenomena, different splits
  5. Luck & Timing: Sometimes the world is not ready (example: Salesforce prompt multi task) and sometimes modeling advances are just ripe for the benchmark

These reflections were mostly inspired by Chris Pott interviewing Sam Bowman on his podcast for the Stanford NLU Class. They go really deep into the creation of some of Sam’s famous benchmarks: The Stanford Natural Language Inference (SNLI) Corpus and the General Language Understanding Evaluation (GLUE) Benchmark and discuss all the aspects above.

In this blog post I want to write about three things: 1) Expand on the list of challenges with case studies mostly from NLP, 2) Look back to the time when I created my own dataset ImageCoDe in 2021 and 3) Compare Vision-and-Language benchmarks with NLP, especially regarding the idea of a benchmark being “solved”.
