I'm a 3rd-year PhD student in AI/NLP at MILA & McGill in Montreal with Siva Reddy, supported by the Vanier Scholarship.
While I am broadly curious about all forms of grounding (for examples, see Experience Grounds Language paper), I have concretely worked on vision-and-language reasoning in the past two years: How can models reason with pragmatic language and visual nuances? Can we repurpose Stable Diffusion for challenging discriminative tasks? I also want to incorporate questions around ethics and society more into my research.

Aside from these intriguing questions, I have organized the NewInML workshop at NeurIPS 2023 and run a reading group on language grounding. In my undergrad, I founded a philosophy society and I am very happy it is still thriving to this day! I believe these kinds of activities outside of publishing papers are crucial to a healthy research environment.

Before coming to Mila, I graduated from LMU Munich in computational linguistics where I worked on relational reasoning and machine translation. I also had great experiences at startups during and after my undergrad, especially when they're small with lots of open questions to explore!

In my free time, I play Ultimate Frisbee competitively and have coached juniors and a mixed-gender team. Nowadays with the PhD it's harder to play on the highest level but I have great memories of playing at U24 Worlds with Germany and several European Club Championships.

If you want to reach out for research or chat about life: benno.krojer@gmail.com
